# Retail Inventory Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview
This project analyzes retail inventory data using SQL server to generate business insights into inventory management, sales performance, pricing strategy, and promotional effectiveness.

---

## Business Objectives

- Monitor inventory levels across products.
- Identify products with critical stock levels.
- Calculate total revenue and units sold.
- Compare sales performance across stores and regions.
- Analyze revenue generated by product category.
- Evaluate the impact of holiday promotions on sales.
- Compare product prices with competitor pricing.
- Classify inventory into stock status categories.

## Key Insights

- The dataset contains **73,101 retailinventory records**
- Inventory levels range from **50 to 500 units**
- **130 products** were identified at the critical inventory level of **50 units**, indicating the need for immediate replenishement.
- Store performance varied based on total revenue and units sold.
- Revenue differed across product categories and regions.
- Holdiay promotion influenced overall sales performance.
- Competitor pricing analysis highlighted pricing differences across categories.
- Product were successfully classified into Critical, Low, Medium, and High stock levels using SWL CASE statements.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Restock products at critical inventory levels to prevent stockouts.
- Increse inventory for high-demand products during peak seasons.
- Continue evaluating promotional campaigns to improve sales performance.
- Monitor competitor pricing regularly to maintain competitive pricing strategies.
- Use regional sales performance to optimize inventory distribution across stores.
